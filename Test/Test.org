** Important Notes
For emacs, the Wolfram REPL use wolframscript -rawterm, where space lines in string are not allowed.
All space lines are automatically removed from strings before running codes.

#+name: sci-wolfram-space-lines-in-string
#+begin_src wolfram
str = "To be, or not to be, that is the question:

Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer

The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune,

Or to take arms against a sea of troubles,

And by opposing end them?";

WriteString["stdout", str, "\n"];
#+end_src

#+RESULTS: sci-wolfram-space-lines-in-string
:results:
To be, or not to be, that is the question:
Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer
The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune,
Or to take arms against a sea of troubles,
And by opposing end them?
:end:

For org version < 9.8, the spaces before a string in session async running will be automatically removed.
For org version >= 9.8, the emacs ~org-babel-comint-async-register~ introduced a new option ~disable-prompt-filtering~ to avoid this situation.
#+begin_src python :session :results output
print("         String with spaces before")
#+end_src

#+RESULTS:
:          String with spaces before

#+begin_src python :session :async :results output
print("         String with spaces before")
#+end_src

#+RESULTS:
: String with spaces before

~WriteString~ vs ~Print~:
~Print~ will automatically truncate strings while ~WriteString~ will keep the original string style.

#+name: sci-wolfram-print-vs-writestring
#+begin_src wolfram
str = "For over three decades, Mathematica has defined the state of the art in technical computing and provided the principal computation environment for millions of innovators, educators, students and others around the world.";
Print[str];
WriteString["stdout", str, "\n"];
#+end_src

#+RESULTS: sci-wolfram-print-vs-writestring
:results:
For over three decades, Mathematica has defined the state of the art in\

>   technical computing and provided the principal computation environment\

>   for millions of innovators, educators, students and others around the\

>   world.
For over three decades, Mathematica has defined the state of the art in technical computing and provided the principal computation environment for millions of innovators, educators, students and others around the world.
:end:
